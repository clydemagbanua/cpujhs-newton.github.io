Virtual Archangel
Shifting the spotlight, heeding the cries of the victims of the injustice system

*Note the game titles are linked to an article

The May 26th killing of George Floyd and the subsequent demonstrations brought much-needed attention to the systemic racial discrimination not just in the United States but in the world. According to DFC Intelligence almost 40% of the population are playing video games. For that reason, giving knowledge while maintaining the fun is possible through the virtual world.

Mafia III

Mafia III is a 2016 action-adventure video game developed by Hangar 13 and published by 2K Games. It is a action-adventure set in an open world and played from a third-person perspective. Set within the fictional city of New Bordeaux (based on New Orleans) in 1968, the story follows former criminal and Vietnam veteran Lincoln Clay, who is forced to return to a life of crime to help his adoptive family settle problems with the local branch of the Mafia.


Assassin's Creed III


Assassin's Creed III is a 2012 action-adventure video game developed by Ubisoft Montreal.The plot is set in a fictional history of real-world events and follows the millennia-old struggle between the Assassins, who fight to preserve peace and free will, and the Templars, who desire peace through control. The framing story is set in the 21st century and features series protagonist Desmond Miles

Watch Dogs 2

Watch Dogs 2 is a 2016 action-adventure game developed by Ubisoft Montreal. Set within a fictionalized version of the San Francisco Bay Area, the game is played from a third-person perspective and its open world is navigated on-foot or by vehicle. Players control Marcus Holloway, a hacker who works with the hacking group DedSec to take down the city's advanced surveillance system known as ctOS.



    1. Promote Teamwork and Communication

    Racial and ethnic differences are evident in other areas. With the help of video games, people found a common goal which disregards the "difference of skin color"


    2. Changes the Perspective on How People View Things

    Having to experience first-hand the discrimination and the different treatment of people, as human beings we would empathize and put ourselves in the different-colored people's shoes. Therefore, changing our perspective on them and may come to a realization on how we are throwing our humanity by judging a person based on their external differences.




    3. Protagonist color don't matter - You are the protagonist of your story

    Some people describes video games as a space whereupon whiteness is centered, lifted up, celebrated and imagined as always the heroic character; whereas other marginalized identities have been pushed aside and only imagined as criminals, terrorists, sexual objects and etc. In these games however, the stereotype has been diminished by centering 'different-colored' characters, storlines, histories as well as preserving and sharing their own culture.
